Quantitative Summary of $A$

Stage I · Singular Value Decomposition: $A = U\,\Sigma\,V^{\dagger}$

The Singular Value Decomposition expresses an arbitrary $m\times n$ matrix as the composition of an input rotation, a diagonal stretch, and an output rotation. QSVT preserves the unitary factors $U$ and $V^{\dagger}$ identically and acts only on the singular values arranged along $\Sigma$; this isolation of the non-unitary content is what makes the entire construction implementable on a quantum register.

Computational procedure (click to expand)
  1. Form the Gram matrix $M = A^{\dagger} A$, which is symmetric positive semidefinite of order $n$.
  2. Diagonalise $M$ via the cyclic Jacobi eigenvalue algorithm to obtain orthonormal eigenvectors and non-negative eigenvalues $\lambda_i$.
  3. Define the singular values $\sigma_i = \sqrt{\lambda_i}$ and assemble $V$ from the eigenvectors of $M$.
  4. Compute the columns of $U$ via $u_i = A v_i / \sigma_i$ for $\sigma_i > 0$, and complete the orthonormal basis by Gram–Schmidt where necessary.
  5. If $m < n$ the procedure is applied to $A^{\dagger}$ and the resulting factors are transposed accordingly.

Stage II · Block Encoding of $A$ within a Unitary Dilation

A non-unitary operator cannot be implemented directly on a quantum register. We therefore embed $A$, after sub-normalisation by a constant $\alpha \ge \lVert A\rVert_2$, as the upper-left block of a larger unitary operator $U_A$ acting on the system together with one or more ancillary qubits. The original matrix is recovered by projecting the ancilla onto $|0\rangle$: $$\bigl(\langle 0^{a}|\otimes I\bigr)\,U_A\,\bigl(|0^{a}\rangle\otimes I\bigr) \;=\; A/\alpha.$$ For an $m\times n$ block $B = A/\alpha$ with $\lVert B\rVert\le 1$, the canonical cosine–sine dilation is $$U_A=\begin{bmatrix} B & \sqrt{I_m - B B^{\dagger}}\\[2pt] \sqrt{I_n - B^{\dagger} B} & -B^{\dagger}\end{bmatrix}\in\mathbb{R}^{(m+n)\times(m+n)}.$$ This construction is exactly unitary whenever $\lVert B\rVert\le 1$.

Verification: the upper-left $m\times n$ block of $U_A$ literally equals $B = A/\alpha$, and direct computation confirms $U_A^{\dagger} U_A = I_{m+n}$.

Stage III · Quantum Signal Processing and the Selection of $P(x)$

Quantum Signal Processing establishes that an alternating sequence of single-qubit rotations $$U_{\vec\phi}(x)=S(\phi_0)\,W(x)\,S(\phi_1)\,W(x)\cdots W(x)\,S(\phi_d)$$ realises any real polynomial $P(x)$ subject to three constraints: $\deg P \le d$, $|P(x)|\le 1$ for $x\in[-1,1]$, and matching parity between $P$ and $d$. The phase angles $\vec\phi$ constitute a classical preprocessing step computed once per polynomial.

The upper-left entry of $\prod_{k} S(\phi_k)\,W(x)$ equals $P(x)$ for the chosen angle sequence.

Determining $\vec\phi$ from $P$ is performed classically using methods such as the Remez algorithm together with root-finding, or the iterative Wilson decomposition. As QSVT inherits $P$ directly from QSP, the visible action of the algorithm is fully characterised by $P$ itself.

Stage IV · Quantum Singular Value Transformation

Replacing the scalar oracle $W(x)$ with the block encoding $U_A$, and replacing each $Z$-rotation with a projector-controlled phase that acts only when the ancilla register is in $|0^a\rangle$, yields the QSVT circuit. The fundamental identity satisfied by this circuit is $$\bigl(\langle 0^{a}|\otimes I\bigr)\,U_{\rm QSVT}\,\bigl(|0^{a}\rangle\otimes I\bigr) \;=\; U\,P\!\bigl(\Sigma/\alpha\bigr)\,V^{\dagger}.$$ Consequently, the entire pipeline reduces operationally to the application of $P$, entry by entry, to the diagonal of the sub-normalised singular value matrix, followed by re-application of the rotations $U$ and $V^{\dagger}$.

Numerical Verification

Three structural invariants are checked on every refresh: orthogonality of $U$ and $V$, non-negativity of $\sigma_i$, and the unitarity of the dilation $U_A$.
